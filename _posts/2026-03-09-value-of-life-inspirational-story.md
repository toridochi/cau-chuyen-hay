# The Value of Life

## A Beggar with One Arm

There once was a beggar who had lost his right arm. His appearance looked pitiful, and almost everyone who saw him gave him money.

One day, he came to a farmer's house asking for food.

The woman of the house said:

“If you move the pile of bricks in front of the door to the backyard, I will give you some money.”

The beggar became angry.

“You can clearly see I only have one arm. How can you ask me to move bricks? Are you making fun of me?”

Without arguing, the woman crouched down and deliberately picked up a brick with one hand.

She carried it to the backyard and returned.

Then she said:

“If I can do it with one hand, why can't you?”

The beggar had nothing to say.

Reluctantly, he began moving the bricks with his single arm.

It took him **two full hours** to finish the job, and he was covered in sweat.

---

## A Lesson in Dignity

The woman handed him a clean white towel.

After wiping his face and neck, the towel turned completely black.

She then gave him **twenty dollars**.

The beggar thanked her repeatedly.

The woman said:

“You don't need to thank me. This is the money you earned with your own sweat.”

The beggar replied:

“I will never forget you. Please allow me to keep this towel as a reminder.”

---

## Years Later

Many years later, the beggar returned.

This time he was wearing a fine suit.

Emotionally, he said to the elderly woman:

“I used to be a beggar. Now I am the chairman of a company.

You helped me regain my dignity and confidence in life. Without you, I might still be wandering and begging.”

The woman smiled and said:

“It wasn't me. You did it yourself.”

The man offered to give her an entire building as a gift.

But she politely refused.

With a smile she said:

“Because everyone in my family has two hands to work with.”

---

## Moral of the Story

The greatest way to help someone is not by giving charity, but by helping them rediscover their dignity and strength.

True change happens when people believe in their own ability to rebuild their lives.

---

## Why This Story Still Matters Today

In modern society, many people struggle with self confidence, unemployment, or difficult circumstances.

This story reminds us that dignity and self respect are powerful forces that can transform a person's life.

Real support does not mean creating dependence. Instead, it means empowering people to stand on their own feet.

When people regain their confidence and sense of purpose, they can achieve far more than anyone expects.
