# The Bottle Beside the Water Pump

## A Man Lost in the Desert

A man had been walking through the desert for two days.

Along the way, he encountered a violent sandstorm. After the storm passed, he completely lost his sense of direction.

Exhausted and nearly dying of thirst, he suddenly discovered an abandoned house.

Dragging his tired body inside, he found a small, poorly ventilated room filled with rotten wood.

In the corner of the room, he noticed something unexpected — a **water pump**.

Excitedly, he rushed forward and tried pumping water.

But no matter how hard he tried, **not a single drop came out**.

---

## The Bottle and the Message

Disappointed, he sat on the ground.

Then he noticed a small bottle sealed with a cork beside the pump. Attached to it was a yellowed note.

The note read:

“You must pour this water into the pump to prime it before it will work.

Don't forget to refill the bottle before you leave.”

He opened the bottle and found that it was indeed **full of water**.

Immediately, a fierce internal struggle began.

If he drank the water himself, he could survive long enough to leave the house.

But if he followed the instructions and poured the water into the pump, there was a risk that **the water might never come back**, and he could die of thirst.

Should he take the risk?

---

## The Decision

After a long moment of hesitation, he made a choice.

He poured **every drop of water** into the old pump.

With trembling hands, he began pumping.

Suddenly…

Water **gushed out in great quantities**.

Relieved, he drank as much water as he needed.

Before leaving, he refilled the bottle, sealed it with the cork, and added his own message to the note:

“Trust me, it works.”

---

## Moral of the Story

Sometimes in life, we must give before we receive.

Trust, generosity, and courage often open the door to greater rewards than playing it safe.

---

## Why This Story Still Matters Today

In modern life, people often struggle between playing it safe and taking risks for greater rewards.

This story reflects a universal challenge: whether we trust the process and invest first, or choose immediate security.

In business, relationships, and personal growth, the principle often remains the same — those who are willing to give first are more likely to receive greater returns later.

It reminds us that trust, courage, and generosity are still powerful forces in today's uncertain world.
