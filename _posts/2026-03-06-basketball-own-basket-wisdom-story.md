# Scoring in Your Own Basket: A Story of Unexpected Wisdom

## An Impossible Situation

In a basketball championship game, Team A faced Team B.

Throughout the tournament, Team A had fought hard, but their chances of advancing were becoming increasingly difficult. To qualify for the next round, they needed to defeat Team B by **at least seven points** in this match.

From the start of the game, both teams played with equal strength. The competition was intense, with constant offense and defense from both sides.

When the game reached the final **five seconds**, Team A was leading by **two points**.

However, to advance to the next round, Team A needed **five more points**.

Anyone familiar with basketball knew that this was almost impossible. Even if Team A managed to score a three point shot in the shortest time possible, the opposing team could easily control the ball and run out the remaining seconds.

Team B could almost see victory ahead. Their players began to show signs of celebration.

At that moment, the head coach of Team A called for a timeout.

After giving his players a few quick instructions, the game resumed.

---

## A Shocking Decision

A player from Team A inbounded the ball from the baseline and moved toward mid court.

Team B, believing victory was already secured, retreated to their defensive half of the court.

Suddenly, the Team A player **turned around**, sprinted toward his own basket, jumped high, and **forcefully dunked the ball into his own team's hoop**.

The crowd erupted in shock.

The referee blew the whistle.

The game ended in a **tie**.

Spectators were completely confused. Many wondered if the player had gone crazy.

---

## The Brilliant Strategy

When overtime began, the audience finally understood the strategy.

By intentionally scoring in their own basket, Team A forced the game into a **five minute overtime period**.

During overtime, Team A played with unstoppable momentum and quickly scored several points.

Meanwhile, Team B seemed mentally shaken by the strange moment they had just witnessed. Their morale dropped, and they struggled to respond.

By the end of overtime, Team A had defeated Team B by **more than seven points** and successfully advanced to the next round.

## Moral of the Story

Experience teaches us to follow common rules.

But in difficult situations, true wisdom sometimes means breaking those rules and thinking creatively.

When facing impossible challenges, unconventional thinking and courage can open new paths to success.
